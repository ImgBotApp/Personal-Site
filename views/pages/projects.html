Current Projects

MYNZ

An augmented-reality game for Apple Watch and iPhone where users can drop virtual traps into the actual world for their friends to run into. Heavily utilizes geographic programming concepts such as geofencing and Apple's Core Location services. Parse powered the backend.

LunchBox

Lunchbox is a mobile app that helps college students find free food events at their campus. Organizations can pay to host their event on the app. Originally founded by David Hepworth and Chase Roberts, I joined the crew to help revamp the app as well as its Django backend.

Group Smart

Groupsmart is a mobile app to help students easily find and create study groups by providing an app specialized to do so, thus providing a single source rather than scrounging around for study groups via social media and word of mouth. Originally founded by myself and my roommate at the time Hunter Kehoe, we built v1.0 of this app for a mobile app contest hosted by the Rollins Center for Entrepreneurship and Technology, in the BYU Marriott School of Management. We won $400 for our entry, and I would now like to improve the app. The backend was in PHP and MySQL but that has since been updated to Django + PostgreSQL. I wrote the native iOS app and Hunter wrote the Android counterpart.
